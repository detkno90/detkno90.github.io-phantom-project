About

Edgar Allan Poe, Lovecraft or Goya himself with his "Black Paintings" are artists who have found beauty in the dark side, in all that is disturbing and bizarre that has to do with hell, evil and the aberrant connecting, thus, with that taste that many of us have for what worries us and causes us uneasiness. When one contemplates the work of Vergvoktre the same question comes to mind as when one sees a film by Cronenberg or Lynch: what kind of sick mind can create something like that?
But, right away you understand that this mental illness is contagious, that you find connections with the strange pleasure that comes from seeing this type of creations that are made without any shame, ready to generate in us strong emotions, dark feelings. Vergvoktre invites us to his particular gallery of horrors in which creepy creatures, condemned people, satanic rites, infinite abysses, tormented ghosts, witches, occultism, disgusting insects and everything related to hell itself coexist. Mixing religion, the eternal struggle between good and evil, decadence and descents into hell in a literal and figurative way in his themes, the Russian artist stabs reality, through visual metaphor, into a rotten society that x-ray without mercy and cruelty. Using charcoal he achieves that dark aesthetic and that ungainly and decadent line that provokes discomfort. Vergvoktre, aware of what his work provokes, adds mystery to his legend with a scarce presence in networks, with which he manages to increase the enigma about his person and about his grotesque concept.
Looking at Vergvoktre's paintings is looking at certain parts of our inner self, that which, perhaps, we are afraid to discover...
